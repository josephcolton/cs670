Birth and Medical Personal

I wanted to see what role the presence of medical personal in the country as well as actually attending the birth played in longer term infant mortality.

I wanted to see if having skilled personnel played a role in infant mortality. It appears that as the percent of skilled personnel drops, infant mortality rises. This indicates a definite correlation with infant mortality.

Results, Analysis, and Discussion

Looking at all of the data and visuals, I see that infant mortality is a complex issue. Clearly, there are signs that sanitation and clean water is related to infant mortality. If I had unlimited money, I would love to perform an experiment where I built sanitation services and clean water facilities in countries without easy access to these services. I would like to then make sure that people had access to sanitation services and clean water, then see how the infant mortality rate was affected. Either way, an improvement in sanitation services and clean water would be a positive change for some people.

The rate at which skilled professionals are present at the birth is correlated with infant mortality and can be used to fairly accurately predict rates, however, causation is hard to establish. It would be nice if the data was more complete and individual cases of infant mortality could be looked at more closely to see if there were direct correlations to skilled professionals. What I have to work with are two aggregate numbers that correlate, but are not directly tied together. It seems like having skilled professionals at the birth is definitely a positive thing and should be encouraged.

I really thought that alcohol and tobacco use would be good predictors of infant mortality, but it seems that is not the case. While I imagine these are tied to overall life expectancy and death from other causes, it does not appear that they significantly lead to infant mortality. The violence against women numbers showed me something I was not fully expecting. I thought it was possible that women who experienced violence from an intimate partner might also have children that experienced that same violence or they might cause it themselves, but I did not expect it to have such a close correlation. The violence numbers worked as a good predictor for infant mortality, which is a sad thing.

My analysis a fairly limited in that I use linear regression instead of more fitted approaches. It is possible that the numbers could have been better predictors if I had used different models. Another limitation was the source of and quality of data that I was using. I would have liked to have modified the data to fix some of the missing numbers by using estimates.

If I were to do this all over, I would have collected and used more data. I would have liked to see things like education levels and other quality of life indicators such as income/GDP. I would have also liked to look at things like food security, political unrest, and birth/birch control policies.

Impact

The data seems to tell a certain story. I used the location and year fields to merge data, but this data could be used to tell other political stories. We have to be careful what conclusions are drawn from the data and analysis.

I identified the following places where I would make recommendations in order to improve infant mortality rates in a given country:

  1. Governments and businesses should invest in improving sanitation and clean water infrastructure. Improving these makes life better and reduced the infant mortality rates.

  2. Governments should encourage more people to become nurses/midwives. They could create training programs and offer scholarships and grants for people in under-served areas who are interested in learning how to become skilled professionals. Then, they should make sure access to skilled professionals is available during pregnancy.

  3. Governments should look at law and social practices that might lead to domestic violence. Ideally, governments and society should frown on domestic violence of any sort and discourage it.

While all of these basically focus on government playing a role, it might need to be done by communities or religions.

While I think my models are fairly accurate, it is possible that they are not accurate. Either way, all of the suggestions I suggested are considered positive things in general, so none of the suggestions really has a negative consequence, with possibly the suggestion about domestic violence.
